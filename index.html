The Sword of Damocles of China's Internet - Exploring the incident in which Netflix's Cat One Cup had its account permanently banned for producing a fake script


  In February 2024, a web celebrity named Cat One Cup became famous overnight for producing and posting a video about picking up a Chinese middle school student's homework in Paris. The video, although full of humor and light-hearted banter, sparked widespread social discussion for its allegedly false information. CatCup later admitted that the content of the video was entirely fictitious, an act that not only led to her permanent ban, but also provoked the public to reflect deeply on online authenticity, legal boundaries, and media responsibility. This paper will explore the complex issues behind this incident, analyze the social impact of disinformation, and the possible long-term effects on online culture.



  • While this sudden and severe penalty has shocked many, there are several questions worth exploring:

    1. Should any content that gains a lot of attention because it is falsely curated be banned? Is what Netflix Cat One Cup did an isolated case?


    2. To a certain extent, moderately false content has the attribute of being widely disseminated and appealing to the public, while blanket blocking of the event because it is false, and treating excessive attention to the atmosphere of the online community, will it be overkill and stifle a large number of excellent original creators?


    3. It is worth noting that in the process of spreading the video of Cat One Cup, the official media did not verify the authenticity of the video before reprinting it and flirting with it, which is one of the aids to expanding the impact of the incident. When CatCup was penalized for faking the video and “consuming social resources”, should the official channel be penalized for its lack of rigour?
